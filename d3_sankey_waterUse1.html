Water Shortages in Third World Countries

The United Nations has long been concerned about the water shortages in Third World Countries. The World Water Development Report by the U.N. in early 2000 states that only 60% of the world's population will have access to its water needs by 2030. In South Asia with a population of over 1.6 billion people, there is a growing concern that population and urban growth has created daily water shortages. People are waiting in queues to obtain drinking water, which has led to citywide protests. There is water everywhere but none to drink since water is used for industrial and agricultural needs. The water shortages also stem from environmental pollution and poor management of this valuable resource. Source: Asia Foundation.
